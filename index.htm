About me ↥

I'm a teaching fellow in syntax at the University of Edinburgh's department of Linguistics and English Language. My research is primarily in theoretical syntax and its interfaces. I am especially interested in ellipsis, and what it can tell us about how (and when) abstract syntactic structures are mapped onto phonological strings.

Topics I've been working on recently include:

  • Implications of strict modularity for the syntax-phonology interface (with Heather Newell)
  • The timing of ellipsis: diagnosing when silence takes hold by looking at how ellipsis disrupts (post-)syntactic operations
  • Semantics-prosody mismatches (with Byron Ahn): when prosodic focus-marking falls outside the domain of semantic focus
  • Dialectal microvariation across English and Scots:
    • Innovative verb second in British English: productive V2 in English and learner-driven theories of language change
    • Exhortative clauses in Scots (with Gary Thoms): what they can tell us about imperative syntax
    • PPs with gaps in (with James Griffiths): pseudopassive-like structures in complex possessive PPs
    • The interaction of prosodic incorporation and copy deletion (with Gary Thoms)
